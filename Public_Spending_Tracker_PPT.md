## SLIDE 2: Feature Overview
### What is the Public Spending Tracker?

This feature monitors how government money is allocated and spent on road projects, enabling citizens to:
- **Track Budget Allocation:** See exactly how much money is assigned to each road
- **Monitor Expenditure:** Track actual spending vs. allocated budget
- **Detect Inefficiencies:** Identify roads with high spending but poor maintenance outcomes
- **Question Misuse:** Flag roads that receive repeated repairs without improvement

**Key Insight:** 
> "If a road has ₹50 lakhs allocated but keeps getting repaired every year, where is the money going?"

## SLIDE 6: Key Metrics Explained

### 🔍 Efficiency Score
**Formula:** How much money spent per accident prevented
- **High Score (80-100):** ✅ Good - Low spending, low accidents
- **Medium Score (50-79):** ⚠️ Monitor - Average efficiency
- **Low Score (0-49):** 🚨 Critical - High spending, many accidents

### 🎯 Inefficiency Flag
**Red flags detected:**
1. Spending > ₹50 lakhs + Accidents > 500 = Possible misuse
2. Road repaired multiple times yearly but still has high accidents
3. Budget allocated but complaints remain high

## SLIDE 8: Why This Matters - 3 Pillars

### 🛑 CORRUPTION DETECTION
- Identifies roads with disproportionate spending
- Flags contractors doing low-quality work
- Reveals patterns of repeated repairs (kickback schemes)

**Example:**
> Road A: ₹1 crore spent, 100 accidents
> Road B: ₹50 lakhs spent, 50 accidents
> Why does Road A cost 2x more with worse outcomes?

### 🔁 INEFFICIENCY IDENTIFICATION
- Spots roads that need different repair approaches
- Identifies design flaws vs. maintenance issues
- Highlights budget misallocations

**Example:**
> Avinashi Road gets repaired yearly but accidents don't decrease
> Suggests structural problem, not just maintenance

### 👀 TRANSPARENCY & ACCOUNTABILITY
- Citizens can see exactly where money goes
- Government contractors become accountable
- Public can demand audits of flagged roads
- Media can investigate suspicious patterns

## SLIDE 9: How Citizens Can Use This

### Step 1: Check Your Area
1. Navigate to "Budget Tracker" (More → Budget Tracker)
2. Select your district from the list
3. View all roads and their spending details

### Step 2: Identify Problems
Look for roads with 🚨 **HIGH RISK** or ⚠️ **MEDIUM RISK** status
- These need citizen attention
- Note the spending vs. outcome mismatch

### Step 3: Take Action
**For High-Risk Roads:**
1. File RTI (Right to Information) request for:
   - Contractor details
   - Work completion reports
   - Quality inspection certificates
2. Submit complaint to municipal corporation
3. Contact local media/NGOs
4. File petition in citizens' forum

**Real Questions to Ask:**
- "Why is ₹1 crore spent but accidents still 1,260?"
- "When was this road last inspected?"
- "Who was the contractor for this project?"
- "What corrective actions are planned?"

## COMMON QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

**Q: How is efficiency calculated?**
A: Cost per accident prevented. Lower cost per accident = higher efficiency.

**Q: Can this detect corruption?**
A: Yes. If Road A costs 2x more than Road B with same outcomes, it flags corruption risk.

**Q: What if a road needs emergency repair?**
A: Emergency repairs show higher spending. The system notes "Last_Maintenance_Date" to identify patterns.

**Q: Who sees this data?**
A: Public. Citizens can access it. Governments should publish it regularly.

**Q: Can this prevent accidents?**
A: Indirectly. By detecting inefficiencies, roads get better maintenance, improving safety.
